The Experience


About The Project

I've attended a PWI since kindergarten, and over the years I noticed how different my experience at school was from those of my white counterparts. In light of feeling this way, I decided to create a project depicting the experiences and thoughts of other POC students at PWIs throughout the Tri-State area.

"I have always been mindful of what I say and do. I don't mind it that much, but a certain side of me is often hidden, and I don't want it to be lost"

B. Moody
Name : Bahsil
School : Riverdale
Grade : 12th
Ethnicity : African-American

“It’s weird being the only black person in the room. Especially when almost everyone you live around looks like you.”

D. Davis
Name : Destin Davis
School : Packer Collegiate Institute
Grade : 11th
Ethnicity : African-American

“It’s very irritating when people blatantly disrespect your existence because they don’t think it’ll offend anyone in the room.”

E.Ovalle
Name : Ellie Ovalle
School : Packer Collegiate Institute
Grade : 12th
Ethnicity : Columbian

“It’s frustrating to be in an environment where I feel so isolated and feel that no one understands me.”

D. Armstrong
Name : Destini Armstrong
School : Packer Collegiate Institute
Grade : 12th
Ethnicity : St.Lucian

“ It’s hard to relate to most of the kids here. They don’t and never will understand the struggle. Being a person of color is another thing to worry about. ”

A. Aly
Name : Aly Aly
School : Packer Collegiate Institute
Grade : 12th
Ethnicity : Egyptian

“ When I was, like, 5 years old, I used to pray to have light skin because I would always hear how pretty that little light skin girl was, or I would hear I was pretty to be dark skin. It wasn't until I was 13 that I really learned to appreciate my skin color and know that I was beautiful.”

Keke Palmer

“ I was an aspiring astrophysicist, and that's how I defined myself, not by my skin color. People didn't treat me as someone with science ambitions. They treated me as someone they thought was going to mug them, or who was a shoplifter. ”

Neil deGrasse Tyson

“I'm looking forward to the day when America will mature to the point that we are a color-blind society. I'm not so sure that in politics that will ever be reality, because politics has a way of separating us based on skin color.”

J.C. Watts
